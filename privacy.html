Georgia Gold Buyers POS ("GGB POS", "we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of our customers and the end-users of our point-of-sale platform. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, store, and protect information when you use GGB POS.

1. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of information through your use of the platform:

  • Customer information: Names, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, and government-issued identification numbers collected at the point of transaction as required by applicable precious metals dealer regulations.
  • Photo ID images: Photographs of government-issued identification documents captured during customer onboarding or transaction processing, stored securely and used solely for compliance verification.
  • Transaction data: Details of buy and sell transactions including metal types, weights, purity, valuations, payout amounts, and payment methods.
  • Inventory data: Records of precious metals inventory including descriptions, valuations, and transfer histories.
  • Account information: Usernames, roles, and hashed PINs for staff accounts within your tenant organization.
  • Usage data: Access logs, audit trails, and system activity for compliance and security purposes.
  • Business information: Business name, location data, and configuration settings provided during account setup.

2. How We Store Your Data

All data is stored using industry-standard security practices:

  • Data is stored on Supabase-backed infrastructure with encryption at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+).
  • Photo ID images and uploaded files are stored in secure object storage with access-controlled URLs.
  • Database backups are performed regularly and stored in encrypted form.
  • Staff PINs are hashed and never stored in plaintext.

3. Multi-Tenant Data Isolation

GGB POS is a multi-tenant platform. Each business account (tenant) operates in a logically isolated data environment. Your customer data, transactions, inventory, and staff records are never accessible to other tenants. All database queries are scoped to your tenant ID, and access controls are enforced at both the application and database layers.

4. How We Use Your Data

  • To provide and maintain the point-of-sale platform and its features.
  • To generate compliance documentation (e.g., IRS Form 8300 reporting).
  • To provide customer support and respond to inquiries.
  • To send service-related notifications (e.g., billing, security alerts).
  • To improve platform performance, reliability, and features using aggregated, anonymized analytics.
  • To comply with applicable laws and regulations governing precious metals dealers.

5. No Sale of Data

We do not sell, rent, trade, or otherwise transfer your data or your customers' data to third parties for marketing, advertising, or any commercial purpose. Data is only shared with third parties when necessary to provide the service (e.g., cloud infrastructure providers) or when required by law.

6. Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide services. Transaction records and compliance-related data (including customer IDs and Form 8300 records) are retained for a minimum of five (5) years in accordance with federal precious metals dealer regulations. Upon account termination, you may request export of your data before deletion is completed. Deletion of tenant data is completed within 90 days of a verified termination request.

7. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your data:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your data, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Portability: Request your data in a machine-readable format. Use the Data Export feature in the platform for transaction and customer records.
  • Objection: Object to certain processing activities.
